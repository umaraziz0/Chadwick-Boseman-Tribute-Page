Summary

Chadwick Aaron Boseman was an American actor who has played several notable roles including Jackie Robinson in 42 (2013), Thurgood Marshall in Marshall (2017), and is most well-known for playing the Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. His Black Panther role won him several awards, including the NAACP Image Award and the Screen Actors Guild Award. Born and raised in South Carolina, Boseman attended Howard University in Washington D.C. where he studied fine arts. He began his early career acting, directing, writing, and teaching in New York City. Boseman died on August 28, 2020, after privately dealing with colon cancer for four years
